About Me

Hey! I am Jose M. Vigil, a First-Generation university student and soon to be graduate, interested in creating and designing technologies that have meaningful impacts on users. My primary focus is in the area of UX Research, which involves understanding and researching who exactly I am creating technology for. I enjoy conducting user research, user interviews, and usability testing whilst creating the best user-centered technologies. Though I am still refining my skills as a UI designer I still enjoy taking all the information I gather through these UX methodologies and strategies and creating strong functional designs that users will enjoy.

Projects

Altru.AI - Course Project

I collaborated with assigned partners on the development of a new LLM designed to assist students and individuals with college applications and the admissions process. I also focused on the user interface and functionality of Altru AI, specifically the ‘Profile Builder,’ which allowed users to input academic and personal details to help the LLM assist with college admission questionnaires and applications. Additionally, I researched and evaluated APIs to facilitate the collection of relevant academic data and university information.

BetterBelli - Course Project

I designed and created a Prototype application focused on dietary restrictions, allowing users to navigate dining options safely, reducing health risks and providing peace of mind for those with unique dietary restrictions. This involved conducting competitive analysis to pinpoint the positives and negatives of similar applications that would help with our creation and design of BetterBelli. I utilized Figma to create low and high-fidelity prototypes of BetterBelli which were used for usability testing and overall functionality testing.

Prenostik's My Learning Coach - Course Project

I collaborated with a partner to develop a web application that helps students understand their academic performance in relation to motivation and mindset. I designed front-end user interfaces in Figma, including webpages, widgets, and data visualizations for both student and instructor views. We implemented interactive and responsive interfaces using React and TypeScript, translating Figma designs into fully functional student and instructor pages.
